History

Learn about the history!

In 87 B.C, the Antikythera mechanism (the oldest known computing device) and thought to be used by the Greeks to calculate astronomical positions to help them navigate through the seas. in 1843, Ada Lovelace wrote the first computer algorithm, in collaboration with Charles Babbage, who devised a theory of the first programmable computer. Ada (the programming language was named after her)

In 1937, the Turing Machine was created by Alan Turing which can basically determine if the program is able to run. In 1954, the transistor was invented by Bell Labs scientists, which made modern-style computing possible. In 1975, Moore’s law came out stating that “every two years, the number of transistors will double.” The more transistors on the circuit board, the faster the processing speed.

Many more things were created, such as the World Wide Web, and the first portable computer. These inventions and improvements continue to bring us to our modern day world, and it is with knowing computer science that we are able to play the games we want, have faster response time, have calculators that can do multiple calculations, have computers that work, and much more!
